# EduVision
EduVision is an open-source, voice-activated desktop assistant specifically conceived to advance digital accessibility for individuals with visual impairments.
The pervasive digitalization of modern life has redefined how individuals engage with information, communicate, and perform professional tasks. However, despite ongoing technological growth, persons with visual impairments continue to experience disproportionate barriers when navigating digital systems. Conventional desktop environments rely primarily on visual cues, creating obstacles for users who depend on auditory or tactile modalities for interaction.
While assistive tools such as screen readers, magnification software, and text-to-speech processors have served as transitional technologies, their efficiency remains constrained by restricted customization, limited integration with desktop-level functions, and the frequent dependence on costly proprietary infrastructure. These challenges become acute when visually impaired users attempt advanced tasks such as managing documents, editing text, or composing lengthy email correspondence, activities that typically demand sequential precision, targeted feedback, and security assurances.
An equally critical limitation lies in existing systems’ inadequate handling of privacy and authentication. Many commercially available voice assistants maintain cloud-hosted data pipelines, thereby exposing user interactions and personal information to potential breaches. This absence of strong, localized security mechanisms poses an ethical and functional concern for users managing confidential personal or institutional data.
EduVision was conceptualized to address these multi-faceted gaps by delivering a comprehensive, voice-governed digital assistant tailored specifically for blind and partially sighted users. Implemented predominantly in Python, the system integrates local and cloud-based components to enable flexible, robust interaction. By combining speech recognition, NLP, and multimodal authentication, EduVision facilitates end-to-end desktop control, effective file management, multilingual summarization, and dynamic content retrieval. With its open-source, modular design, the system offers affordability, transparency, and adaptability—key factors in extending accessibility to educational institutions, developing regions, and low-resource communities.
The remainder of this paper systematically presents Edu Vision’s development, architecture, evaluation, and implications within the broader context of inclusive computing research
